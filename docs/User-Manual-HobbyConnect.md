## 1. Introduction

HobbyConnect is a university hobby and skill community platform designed for Rangsit University students. It allows students to discover peers who share similar hobbies, join or create hobby communities, chat in real time, organize events, and stay informed through university announcements — all in one place.

### User Roles

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Student (User)** | The default role. Can join communities, participate in chat and events, and manage their profile. |
| **Community Leader** | The creator of a community. Can manage community activities and submit announcement posts for admin review. |
| **Administrator** | Has full platform oversight. Can publish announcements, review pending posts, ban/unban users, and delete inappropriate content. |

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 System Requirements

- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari).
- An active internet connection.
- A valid **Rangsit University email address** (`@rsu.ac.th`).

### 2.2 Creating an Account

1. Open HobbyConnect in your browser.
2. On the login page, click **"Create account"** at the bottom.
3. Fill in the registration form:
   - **Student ID** — Your university student ID (7–10 digits).
   - **First Name** and **Last Name**.
   - **University Email** — Must end with `@rsu.ac.th`.
   - **Password** — At least 8 characters.
   - **Confirm Password** — Re-enter the same password.
4. Click **"Create account"**.
5. You will see a confirmation message instructing you to check your email.

### 2.3 Confirming Your Email

After registering:

1. Open your university email inbox.
2. Look for a confirmation email from HobbyConnect.
   - If you do not see it, check your **spam or junk** folder.
3. Click the confirmation link in the email.
4. You will be redirected to HobbyConnect and signed in automatically.

If the email does not arrive, return to the registration success screen and click **"Resend confirmation email"**.

### 2.4 Logging In

1. Navigate to the login page.
2. Enter your **University Email** and **Password**.
3. Click **"Sign in"**.
4. If your credentials are correct, you will be taken to the home page.

If you see a message that your account has been restricted, an administrator has banned your account. Contact support for assistance.

## 3. Onboarding

When you log in for the first time, you will be guided through the onboarding process:

1. You will see the **"Welcome to HobbyConnect"** screen.
2. Select one or more **interest categories** that match your hobbies. Available categories include:
   - Sports
   - Tech/Coding
   - Arts & Music
   - Study Groups
   - Science & Research
   - Languages & Culture
   - Gaming
   - Fitness & Wellness
3. For each selected category, choose your **skill level**:
   - **Noob** — Just starting out.
   - **Skilled** — Comfortable and experienced.
   - **Pro** — Expert level.
4. Click **"Complete Onboarding"** (or the submit button).
5. You will be redirected to the home page.

You must select at least one interest to proceed.

## 4. Navigating the Application

HobbyConnect uses a sidebar-based layout with a top header bar.

### 4.1 Sidebar

The sidebar is your primary navigation menu. It contains:

- **Uni Announcement** — Takes you to the home feed (global announcements).
- **My Profile** — Opens your profile page.
- **Create Community** — Opens a modal to create a new hobby community.
- **Your Communities** — Lists communities you have created or joined. Click any community name to open it.
- **Sign Out** — Logs you out and returns to the login page.

On mobile devices, the sidebar is hidden by default. Tap the **menu icon** (hamburger button) in the top-left corner of the header to open it.

### 4.2 Header

The header bar at the top of the screen provides:

- **Menu button** (mobile) — Opens the sidebar.
- **Search bar** — Search for communities by name. Results appear in a dropdown as you type.
- **Notifications bell** — Shows the count of unread notifications. Click to expand the notification dropdown.
- **Theme toggle** — Switch between light mode and dark mode.

### 4.3 Dark / Light Theme

Click the **sun/moon icon** in the header to toggle between light mode and dark mode. Your preference is saved in your browser and persists across sessions.

## 5. Uni Announcement (Home Feed)

The home page displays the **Uni Announcement** feed — a list of published global announcements.

- Announcements are displayed in reverse chronological order (newest first).
- Each announcement card shows:
  - **Date** of publication.
  - **Title** and **body text**.
  - **Image** (if attached).
- Author information is **not shown** on announcements by design.
- The feed is paginated — scroll down to load more announcements.

Only administrators can publish announcements directly. Community leaders can submit posts for admin review (see [Section 11](#11-community-leader-guide)).

## 6. Communities

Communities are hobby-based groups where students can interact through chat, organize events, and connect with peers who share similar interests.

### 6.1 Finding Communities

There are several ways to discover communities:

- **Search bar** (header) — Type a community name to search. Click a result to open the community page.
- **Your Communities** (sidebar) — Browse communities you have already joined or created.
- **Explore from other users' profiles** — View communities that other members are part of.

### 6.2 Joining and Leaving a Community

- On a community page, click the **"Join"** button to become a member.
- Once joined, the button changes to **"Leave"**. Click it to leave the community.
- Joining grants access to the community's **Chat**, **Events**, and **Members** tabs.

### 6.3 Creating a Community

1. In the sidebar, click **"Create Community"**.
2. A modal dialog will appear. Fill in:
   - **Title** — The name of your community.
   - **Description** — A brief description of the community's purpose.
   - **Category** — Select the most relevant category.
3. Click **"Create"** to create the community.
4. The new community will appear under **"Your Communities"** in the sidebar.
5. As the creator, you are automatically the community leader with management privileges.

### 6.4 Community Chat

The **Chat** tab within a community allows real-time messaging:

- Messages appear in chronological order.
- Type your message in the input field at the bottom and press **Enter** or click **Send**.
- New messages from other members appear instantly (real-time updates).
- Community leaders and administrators can moderate chat by removing messages or banning disruptive users.

You must be a member of the community (or have privileged access) to view and participate in chat.

### 6.5 Community Events

The **Events** tab allows members to view and create community events:

- **Viewing events** — See a list of upcoming and past events with details such as title, location, date, capacity, and required skill level.
- **Creating an event** — Fill out the event creation form with:
  - **Title**
  - **Location**
  - **Date and time**
  - **Capacity** (maximum number of participants)
  - **Required skill level** (optional)

Banned members cannot view or create events.

### 6.6 Members Tab

The **Members** tab displays a list of all community members, including:

- The community creator (leader).
- All joined members with their profile information and interests.
- Click on a member's name to view their public profile.

## 7. Profile Management

### 7.1 Editing Your Profile

1. Click **"My Profile"** in the sidebar, or navigate to `/profile`.
2. You will see a form with your profile information.
3. Editable fields include:
   - **First Name** and **Last Name**
   - **Bio** — A short description about yourself.
   - **Major** — Your field of study.
4. After making changes, click **"Save"** to update your profile.

Your **Student ID** and **Email** are displayed for reference but cannot be changed.

### 7.2 Avatar

- On the profile page, you can choose an avatar using the **Avatar Picker**.
- Select from the available avatar options.
- Your avatar is shown across the platform (sidebar, chat messages, member lists).

### 7.3 Skills

The Skills section lets you showcase your abilities:

1. In the **Skills** section of your profile, type a skill name or pick from suggestions.
2. Select a skill level: **Noob**, **Skilled**, or **Pro**.
3. Click to add the skill.
4. You can remove existing skills by clicking the remove button next to each one.
5. Click **"Save"** to persist your changes.

### 7.4 Hobby Tags

Hobby tags are free-text labels that describe your hobbies:

1. In the **Hobbies** section, type a hobby name or pick from suggestions.
2. Press Enter or click to add the hobby tag.
3. Remove tags by clicking the remove button on each tag.
4. Click **"Save"** to persist your changes.

### 7.5 Changing Your Password

1. On the profile page, click the **"Change Password"** section to expand it.
2. Enter your **Current Password** for verification.
3. Enter your **New Password** (at least 8 characters).
4. Enter **Confirm New Password**.
5. Click **"Change Password"** to update.
6. A success or error message will be displayed.

## 8. Viewing Other Users' Profiles

You can view other users' public profiles by clicking their name in:

- Community member lists.
- Chat messages.

A public profile shows:

- Name and avatar.
- Bio and major.
- Skills and skill levels.
- Hobby tags.

## 9. Notifications

HobbyConnect provides in-app notifications to keep you informed:

- The **bell icon** in the header shows the number of unread notifications.
- Click the bell to open the notification dropdown.
- From the dropdown you can:
  - View recent notifications.
  - **Mark all as read** to clear the unread count.
  - **Clear all** to dismiss all notifications.

On larger screens (xl breakpoint), a **right-side panel** also displays notification summaries and quick tips.

## 10. Password Recovery

If you forget your password:

1. On the login page, click **"Forgot password?"**.
2. Enter your university email address and click **"Send reset link"**.
3. Check your email inbox (and spam folder) for a password reset link.
4. Click the link — you will be redirected to the **Reset Password** page.
5. Enter your new password and confirm it.
6. Click **"Reset Password"** to save your new password.
7. You will be signed in and redirected to the home page.

## 11. Community Leader Guide

When you create a community, you become its **Community Leader**. This grants you additional capabilities within that community.

### 11.1 Submitting Announcement Posts

Community leaders can submit posts to the Uni Announcement feed for admin review:

1. Navigate to your community page.
2. Click the **"Submit Post"** button (visible only to the community creator).
3. Fill in the post form:
   - **Title** — A concise headline.
   - **Content** — The full announcement body text.
   - **Image** (optional) — Attach an image (maximum **500 KB**).
4. Click **"Submit"**.
5. The post is saved with a status of **PENDING_REVIEW**.
6. An administrator must approve it before it appears in the Uni Announcement feed.

### 11.2 Managing Your Community

As a community leader, you have moderator-level access to your community:

- Full access to the **Chat**, **Events**, and **Members** tabs.
- Ability to moderate chat messages within your community.
- Ability to manage events.

## 12. Administrator Guide

Administrators have full oversight over the platform. This section covers admin-specific features.

### 12.1 Admin Mode Toggle

Admins have a **View Mode** toggle in the sidebar:

- **User Mode** — The admin sees the app like a regular user. The Admin dashboard link is hidden, and admin-specific actions are disabled.
- **Admin Mode** — Enables the Admin dashboard link and activates admin capabilities across the platform (e.g., seeing all communities, accessing privileged features).

Toggle the mode using the switch in the sidebar. The selected mode persists across page reloads.

### 12.2 Admin Dashboard Overview

To access the Admin dashboard:

1. Ensure **Admin Mode** is active (toggle in the sidebar).
2. Click **"Admin"** in the sidebar.
3. The dashboard displays several management sections:
   - **Users** — Full list of registered users.
   - **Pending Posts** — Posts submitted by community leaders awaiting review.
   - **Published Posts** — Currently published announcements.
   - **Recent Messages** — Latest chat messages across all communities.
   - **Recent Events** — Latest events across all communities.

A **search bar** at the top of the dashboard lets you filter users by name or email.

### 12.3 Managing Users (Ban / Unban)

From the **Users** section of the Admin dashboard:

1. Find the user you want to manage (use the search bar to filter).
2. Click **"Ban"** next to a user to restrict their account.
   - Banned users are signed out immediately and see a restriction notice when trying to log in.
3. Click **"Unban"** to restore a user's access.

You cannot ban yourself (your own account is excluded from the ban action).

### 12.4 Reviewing Pending Posts

From the **Pending Posts** section of the Admin dashboard:

1. View the list of posts submitted by community leaders with status **PENDING_REVIEW**.
2. Each pending post shows the title, content, author name, and submission date.
3. For each post, you can:
   - Click **"Approve"** — Changes the status to **PUBLISHED**, making it visible in the Uni Announcement feed.
   - Click **"Reject"** — Changes the status to **REJECTED**. The post will not be published.

### 12.5 Managing Published Posts

From the **Published Posts** section of the Admin dashboard:

1. View all currently published announcements.
2. Click **"Delete"** next to any post to permanently remove it from the feed.

### 12.6 Moderating Messages and Events

From the Admin dashboard:

- **Recent Messages** — View the latest chat messages across all communities. Click **"Delete"** to remove inappropriate messages.
- **Recent Events** — View the latest events across all communities. Click **"Delete"** to remove inappropriate events.

### 12.7 Publishing Announcements

Administrators can publish announcements directly (without review):

1. Ensure **Admin Mode** is active.
2. On the **home page** (Uni Announcement), click the **"Create Post"** button (visible only to admins in Admin Mode).
3. Fill in the post form:
   - **Title** — A concise headline.
   - **Content** — The full announcement body text.
   - **Image** (optional) — Maximum **500 KB**.
4. Click **"Publish"**.
5. The announcement is saved with status **PUBLISHED** and immediately appears in the feed.

## 13. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I didn't receive my confirmation email. What should I do?**
A: Check your spam or junk folder. University email systems sometimes delay or filter automated messages. You can click "Resend confirmation email" on the registration success screen.

**Q: Can I register with a non-university email?**
A: No. HobbyConnect requires a valid Rangsit University email address ending with `@rsu.ac.th`.

**Q: Can I change my Student ID or email after registering?**
A: No. Your Student ID and email are set during registration and cannot be modified.

**Q: How do I switch between light and dark mode?**
A: Click the sun/moon icon in the top-right area of the header bar.

**Q: How do I leave a community?**
A: Navigate to the community page and click the **"Leave"** button.

**Q: Why can't I see the Chat or Events tab in a community?**
A: You need to be a member of the community. Click **"Join"** first, then the tabs become accessible.

**Q: What happens if I'm banned?**
A: You will be signed out immediately and see a notification that your account has been restricted. Contact a platform administrator if you believe this was done in error.

**Q: What is the maximum image size for posts?**
A: Images attached to posts must be **500 KB or smaller**.

**Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?**
A: On the login page, click **"Forgot password?"** and follow the instructions to receive a password reset link via email.

**Q: How do I become a Community Leader?**
A: Creating a community automatically makes you its leader. Any student can create a community via the sidebar.

**Q: How do I become an Administrator?**
A: Administrator roles are assigned at the database level by existing administrators. This is not a self-service operation.
